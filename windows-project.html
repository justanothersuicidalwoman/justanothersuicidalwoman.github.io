Windows Project

Waiting for inspiration is what holds writing from being born. days can pass by faster than imagined without any words being written because we don't find the right topic or right circumstances. In the method of uncreative writing two things are clear: one, there is no more time to wait to know what to write, because writing is a practice and practise needs structure to happen withough even having ideas. two, there are enough stuff available around us that we can make writing from that sometimes we don't even have to make new things, it is enough to work with what already exists. I used to be a shopping addict, but now shopping centers, malls and “stuff, shitloads of stuff” makes me sick. I am more of a collector now, and, still, a writer. Collecting and writing are the first tools of uncreative writing. In the uncreative writing: windows project I attempt to take text from the city and specialy window to the houses in the Netherlands and turn the city into pages of a book — In a collective, we look through window and from the window to the inner space or from inside to the outer space and look at windows as "quotations"; where text exists in between frames. Uncreativley inspired by Walter Benjamin Arcades Projects, I call this, Windows Project. Many other thinkiers and writers are uncreatively collaged into this: like Kenneth Goldsmith's uncreative writing, Unoriginal Genius from Marjiorie Perloff. This might be a refusal to create, or poetry of what already exists, or a failure of writing. but one thing is clear: we won't wait to write, we write to practice.
